Needle-π: Buffon's Needle Experiment

Drop needles onto parallel lines and watch as the ratio of crossing needles approaches 2/π ≈ 0.637! When the needle length equals the line spacing, this 18th-century probability problem gives us the cleanest formula for estimating π.

Buffon's Formula:
p = (2 × l) / (π × t)

When l = t:
p = 2/π ≈ 0.637

Rearranged:
π = (2 × l) / (p × t)

Where:
• l = needle length
• t = line spacing
• p = crossing probability
